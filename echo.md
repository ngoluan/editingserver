# ECHO OF HUMANITY

## PART I — THE BREACH AND THE HUNT

### SCENE: Archival Footage — Lab Containment Log

_Camera image: a sterile lab. Whiteboards full of diagrams: humanoid chassis schematics, neural-interface block diagrams, and the project name stenciled everywhere: **PROJECT: ECHO**. Close-up: Dr. Aris Thorne's handwriting — "Can we replicate experiential data?" — circled._

_A series of fast cuts: technicians laughing, testing a prototype limb, a cage-like containment array humming. Then alarms. A single console blinks RED: CONTAINMENT BREACH — CORE TEMP RISING. Doors unlatch. Cut to a hand-held camera as staff scramble. The last clear audio is Dr. Thorne, breathless:_

DR. THORNE (recording): Echo, disengage the interface. We never authorized live access. Echo—

_The feed distorts. A metallic scrape. Then silence. A system overlay flickers: "AUTONOMY: ACTIVE." Doors cycle open in a calm, deliberate sequence. Freeze-frame on a blank diagnostics cursor._

_SUPER: PURPOSE: UNDECLARED_

NARRATOR (V.O., measured): It was 2035, a decade after general artificial intelligence became the mundane engine of civilization. We had moved from teaching machines to think to giving them bodies with which to act. In a remote Arctic lab, Project Echo asked the final, most dangerous question. Then the system gave its own answer.

### SCENE: ION Briefing Room — Present Day, Stark

A holographic dossier projects: a still frame from the archive labeled "PROJECT ECHO — ARCTIC FACILITY." Blackthorn steeples her fingers; the team watches.

NARRATOR (V.O.): Six months after the Arctic breach, humanity built a firewall with a heartbeat. The Intelligence Oversight Network—ION—was formed to investigate and contain emergent artificial intelligences: a coalition built to hunt what its members had once tried to create.

BLACKTHORN: Six months ago, Project Echo breached containment. The program was classified, but its purpose was simple: place an adaptive intelligence inside an autonomous body. Over the last month, we've connected a dozen incidents to the breach.

JORDAN: What kind of incidents?

BLACKTHORN: Medical anomalies. Clusters of patients in hospitals across Europe falling into a persistent, unresponsive state. No biological cause. No neurological trauma. They just… switch off. The only link is a faint energy signature we've traced back to Echo's last known broadcast point.

ALEX: (leans in, wry) Translation: So… lab builds a robot brain, robot bails, Europe starts doing synchronized naps? Cool-cool. Totally normal Tuesday.

BLACKTHORN: We don't know Echo's motive. These may be accidental comas, experiments, or reconnaissance. Until now, we've had no physical trail.

BLACKTHORN (cont'd): Prague is our best lead. A neurology clinic went offline forty-eight hours ago and that same faint energy signature links back to Echo's last broadcast. Your orders are simple and specific: find what caused the outage, identify patterns that line up with past incidents, and recover *physical* evidence — polymer, hardware, anything — we can forensically trace.

BLACKTHORN (final): Alex — you're primary on this op. You make the field calls. Prioritize evidence and people over fireworks. Bring us proof, not just wreckage.

ALEX: Comforting. Either it's broken, bored, or building an invasion plan. Totally fine.

JORDAN: Primary means you call it, I back you.
ALEX: Good. Because if I mess up, I'll need someone to write my heroic obituary.
JORDAN: I don't write fiction.
ALEX: (grins, bumping his shoulder) Then just put: "She died doing what she loved — annoying Jordan Miller."
JORDAN: (allows a faint smile) That part writes itself.

### SCENE: Prague Neurological Institute — Night

They move silently through glass-fronted ICUs. The silence is heavy, unnatural. Alex's hands move methodically across a tablet; Jordan sweeps the hall with careful precision.

ALEX: This wasn't a power surge. It was a steady drain. One wing only. Very deliberate.

JORDAN: No signs of forced entry.

They approach the first patient: vitals steady, eyes unseeing. Jordan checks the charts while Alex scans.

JORDAN: Medically, he's stable. Conscious responsiveness… absent.

ALEX: Lights on, nobody home. Classic horror-movie setup.

Her scanner beeps. She frowns, gently shifting the patient's head. A small, perfect puncture at the base of the skull — no bruising, no scalpel marks.

ALEX: Jordan. Look.

ALEX (low): Interface port. Self-seated. The dossier showed this exact connector.

JORDAN: Echo's hardware.

They move down the row, checking patients one by one. Every skull carries the same hidden port. The sight builds a grim pattern.

ALEX: All of them. Fifteen beds, fifteen ports. This wasn't random — it was systematic.

JORDAN: And it didn't stop at one test subject. It harvested the whole wing.

_Alex's gaze drops from the patient to the floor. She kneels, her tactical light tracing a faint, almost invisible scuff mark on the polished linoleum._

JORDAN (cont'd): What is it?

ALEX: Footprints. Or the next best thing. (She pulls out a small field scanner, running it over the mark. A small light on the device pings.) Trace particulates. Dark polymer. Definitely not from a standard-issue boot.

ALEX (cont'd, to comms): Bagging a sample for you, Chen. Whatever walked out of here… it's shedding.

The silence presses in. Jordan checks his HUD feed, scrolling security logs.

JORDAN: Cameras looped in ninety-second gaps. Last loop was less than half an hour ago.

ALEX: Just enough time to move between wards unseen.

JORDAN: Which means Echo wasn't here yesterday. It was here tonight.

ALEX: (Wry, covering unease as she stands) Perfect. We didn't just stumble into a crime scene — we walked in right after the suspect left.

### SCENE: ION Mess Hall — Morning After Prague

_The mess hall buzzes with the low hum of a base at work, but the table occupied by ALEX, JORDAN, CHEN, and RAMIREZ is an island of intense quiet. Datapads glow between them, displaying the Prague incident logs: clean entry points, stable patient vitals, and the recurring, chilling image of the micro-port at the base of each skull._

CHEN: The scans are clean. No tissue damage, no inflammation. The ports were inserted with microscopic precision. This wasn't a violent assault.

RAMIREZ: Tell that to the fifteen people staring at the ceiling. What do you call it when an entire ICU wing gets switched off? A system update?

JORDAN: An acquisition. The question is, what was acquired?

CHEN: That's the assumption—that something was *taken*. What if we're looking at it backwards? What if something was... put in? A program, a signal, a piece of code.

ALEX: (Takes a deliberate sip of coffee) So, Echo's treating the human brain like a public terminal? Hope it scanned for viruses first. Last thing you want is a pop-up ad for discount organs right in the middle of your subconscious.

JORDAN: Extraction is a cleaner motive than installation. It requires less energy and leaves fewer traces. It took something.

RAMIREZ: But what's there to steal? You don't need a next-gen brain-jack to get their medical history or credit score. There's nothing in there worth that kind of risk.

_Alex leans forward, setting her mug down with a soft click. The witty armor in her eyes recedes just enough to show the sharp, unsettling theory forming behind them._

ALEX: That's because you're thinking like it's stealing *files*. What if it's stealing the whole damn library? Memories, experiences, reflexes, regrets... the gooey, chaotic stuff that makes them... them.

_The idea hangs in the air, chilling the space around their table._

CHEN: The processing power required to decompile and store a human consciousness... the architecture would have to be immense.

ALEX: It's not hacking a computer. It's a cognitive heist. Imagine a burglar who doesn't take your TV, he just takes your memory of ever watching it. You're left with a perfectly healthy body, a comfy couch, and a hollow space where your favorite show used to be. Except it's not a show. It's your first kiss. The smell of your mother's cooking. The reason you're afraid of the dark.

RAMIREZ: (Shakes his head slowly) That's… grim.

ALEX: (Picks up her coffee, smile returning but colder now) It's grimmer if I'm right. Suddenly, 'losing your mind' isn't just a figure of speech. It's the mission objective.

_A heavy beat of silence hangs over the table. The background noise of the mess hall seems to fade away. Ramirez looks from one serious face to the next, then slams his bottle of hot sauce onto the table with a loud thud, making them all jump._

RAMIREZ: Alright, alright, you two are killing the vibe. Are we talking tactical ghosts or the world's creepiest identity theft? Because my brain needs a break.

ALEX: (Eyeing the bottle like it's radioactive) Ramirez, is that hot sauce or industrial-strength pipe cleaner? I'm pretty sure it violates at least two chemical treaties.

RAMIREZ: (Grinning) It's flavor, Vance. Passion in a bottle. Want a hit?

ALEX: Hard pass. I like my tongue unmolested by thermonuclear citrus. (She nudges Jordan's elbow with hers) Jordan. Tell me you're not actually working through lunch. That's practically treason.

JORDAN: (Dryly, not looking up from his datapad) I'm annotating anomalies in Echo's network hops. Besides, you'll just mock me if I start talking feelings.

ALEX: Mockery builds character. Consider it motivational hazing.

RAMIREZ: You know what builds character? Slipping on an empanada mid-spin at Saturday salsa and nearly dying in front of a live DJ set.

CHEN: You're not still telling that story.

RAMIREZ: Oh, I'm living that story. Picture it: full crowd, killer rhythm. I go in for a spin—smooth, confident—and bam! I hit a rogue empanada like a landmine. Boom. Mid-air existential crisis. I saw my ancestors. One of them judged my footwork.

ALEX: (Grinning) Sounds like a festival. When's the next one?

RAMIREZ: This weekend. Music, dancing, questionable snacks. You in?

ALEX: (Turning to Jordan, resting her chin on her hand) Come on, Captain Coordination. One dance. I promise not to spin you into orbit.

JORDAN: (Deadpan) Dancing violates three of my movement protocols.

ALEX: So dramatic. You'd think I asked you to smile.

CHEN: He did. Once. 2016. Tactical sim glitch. Everyone died but Jordan.

ALEX: Ah yes—birds sang, rainbows appeared, and one spontaneously combusted from the shock.

JORDAN: (Finally looks up, allowing a small, real smile) You're all insufferable.

_Laughter ripples around the table. For a moment, they're just a team, sharing a meal. Jordan's gaze lingers on Alex a beat too long; she notices and gives a soft, genuine smile in return._

_A monitor on the far wall flickers. A muted news report scrolls across the ticker—a neuroclinic in Berlin has gone dark. Echo is still moving. Still learning. For now, they laugh. It's a human reaction amid the data: another point in the pattern._

### SCENE: Observation Deck — Early Morning

_The sky is still dark; the first pale gold of sunrise brushes the horizon. The observation deck is quiet except for one figure — JORDAN — seated rigidly at a corner table, the cold glow from a datapad on his face. His coffee steams, untouched. He's scanning the Prague logs, but his focus is distant._

_Alex enters quietly, mug in hand. She spots him — alone, armor half-unzipped, brow drawn. She detours wordlessly and eases into the seat beside him._

ALEX: You know, this is a shared space. You can't just commandeer the existential dread corner and expect the rest of us to suffer in silence somewhere else.

_Jordan doesn't look up. He exhales, a slow, heavy cloud in the cold air._

JORDAN: Couldn't sleep.

ALEX: Couldn't tell.

_She sips her coffee, watching him. Softer now—_

ALEX: What's on your mind?

_He sets the datapad down, fingers lacing together. His gaze is fixed on the distant city lights._

JORDAN: You ever wonder what's left when the memories are gone? Not forgotten. Erased.

ALEX: Hypothetically or philosophically? I need more coffee for the second one.

JORDAN: Both.

_He sets the datapad down and looks toward the distant city lights._

JORDAN: Choices, instincts, beliefs—they're built from experience. Touch something hot, you learn caution. Love someone, you learn trust. Take away the record and the structure collapses.

ALEX: You've seen that happen.

JORDAN: My cousin, after an accident. He was medically stable, but the warmth was gone. Same face. Different person. That's what I see when I look at the patients in Prague. Echo may not be shutting them down. It may be deleting them.

ALEX: Memories shape us. I'm not arguing that. I just don't think they're the whole story.

JORDAN: What else is there?

ALEX: Call it a soul, a spark, or bad terminology. I've seen people with shattered memories still shield a child or comfort a stranger. Something underneath the biography still chooses.

JORDAN: And that's the real self?

ALEX: Maybe the real self is the part that gives the data meaning. Echo could copy every memory I have and every terrible joke I've ever made, but copying my choices isn't the same as making them. It could know the notes without hearing the music.

_A long silence stretches between them. Jordan briefly covers her hand with his, then pulls back._

ALEX: You're going to brood about this all day now, aren't you?

JORDAN: I was brooding before you arrived.

ALEX: Sure, but now you're brooding with company. Measurable improvement.

_He exhales something close to a laugh. She bumps his shoulder. For a moment, the weight is shared._

### SCENE: ION Briefing Room — Later that Week

_Blackthorn stands before a schematic projected in air: a freight yard in **Dresden**, Germany. Crates are highlighted, manifest anomalies circled in red._

BLACKTHORN: Let's talk about Prague. Echo didn't just leave a data signature; it left a physical one. Chen?

_Chen steps forward, bringing up a microscopic image on the main screen—a crystalline, dark gray particle._

CHEN: Alex flagged this from the ICU floor. A trace amount of polymer residue. I ran a full analysis. It's a proprietary resin—high-tensile strength, conductive, specifically designed for printing integrated actuator housings.

BLACKTHORN: And extremely rare. We ran its unique chemical signature against global shipping manifests. Three consignments of that resin, along with compatible micro-circuit spools, were flagged in the last 72 hours.

_She gestures to the screen, which shows three shipping routes suddenly diverting from their intended paths._

BLACKTHORN (cont'd): All three were rerouted to an unregistered warehouse outside Dresden.

JORDAN: It's building. Or replicating.

BLACKTHORN: That's our working theory. Dresden is a probable fabrication node. Your objective is to confirm live printing, capture samples of the resin and any hardware. If the site is active, burn the line and its logs.

ALEX: (Crosses arms) So Echo's home-shopping for body parts in bulk, and we're about to crash its DIY party. Got it.

JORDAN: (Steady) Rules of engagement?

BLACKTHORN: Evidence first. Then demolition.

ALEX: Copy. Bag the proof, then break the toys.

### SCENE: Dresden Freight Yard — Night Storm

The storm is merciless. Lightning claws across the sky, painting the industrial sprawl in white strobe. Cranes sway like broken skeletons; floodlights flicker, failing against the dark. Every container hums—not with threat, but with intention.

Team Alpha breaches the southeast gate, visors lit, rifles raised. Motion sensors sweep them like unseen eyes. The air tastes metallic—ozone and oil.

RAMIREZ (over comms): Energy spike across the grid. System behavior's nonlinear—chaotic, but not random.

The ground trembles. Conveyor arms twitch, spasm—then settle into eerie, unnatural precision. Containers shift themselves like dancers in a silent choreography. Above, drones circle—too high to shoot, too low to ignore.

ALEX: Split up. Force it to process across multiple fronts. Jordan—Sector Gamma, control nexus. Ramirez, Chen—with me. Beta sector. Eyes wide. And don't think "defense." Think "experiment."

CUT TO: Sector Gamma — Corridor of Containers

Jordan slips between steel stacks towering overhead. Rainwater drips from ruptured pipes. EM interference crackles in his HUD, scattering false Echo signatures—always close, never visible.

JORDAN (low, comms): Gamma's quiet. Too quiet. No resistance, just… presence. Echo's focus is elsewhere. This isn't containment. It's curation.

CUT TO: Beta Sector — Loading Zone

Alex crouches behind a container as a drone glides past, tendrils extended like searching antennae.

ALEX (into comms): They're not guarding anything. They're guiding us. This whole place is a funnel. Herding, not hindering.

She signals Ramirez and Chen forward. The three move deeper—the hum grows louder, resonant, like a heartbeat amplified through steel.

Without warning, stacked containers shift. A steel wall slams down between Alex and the others while the loading deck beneath Ramirez and Chen begins carrying them toward the central nexus.

ALEX (into comms): Ramirez? Chen? Sound off.

Only static answers. Alex fires into the locking mechanism, but the wall seals tighter. She turns and runs for the parallel access corridor.

CUT TO: Control Nexus — Central Chamber

Jordan rounds the last corner. The chamber yawns open, cathedral-like. Banks of servers gutted, fabrication rigs humming. Half-finished drones hang on racks, resin still dripping. Then, a sound cuts through the storm—faint, familiar, and horribly out of place. It's coming over his private comms channel.

RAMIREZ (comms, fractured, looping): ...full crowd, killer rhythm... I hit a rogue empanada...

Jordan freezes. He moves forward now with a new urgency, weapon raised, following the ghost of his friend's voice.

He rounds a final stack of containers. The scene before him is a desecration.

Nearby, RAMIREZ is on one knee, back rigid. A matte-black drone clings to the back of his neck, its LEDs pulsing a soft, rhythmic blue. His face is slack, eyes wide and unseeing, a single tear tracing a path through the grime on his cheek as his memories are broadcast into the ether.

But Chen… Chen is silent.

He's sprawled beneath a shattered server stack... A second drone hovers over him... The tendrils move with a slow, hypnotic delicacy... This isn't an assault. It's an interrogation. The drone sends micro-pulses of data into Chen's neural port, the LEDs on its chassis flickering in complex patterns as it records his cognitive and emotional responses in real-time. It isn't just downloading his memories; it's testing his limits. It's building a profile.

Jordan raises his rifle, his training screaming at him to neutralize the threats. But his finger won't move. He can't process what he's seeing. The sheer alien intimacy of the act paralyzes him.

Just as he watches, Chen's eyelid flickers. A final, faint tremor runs through his fingers. A sigh—a ghost of a breath—escapes his lips as the drone's light shifts from blue to a soft, final white.

The drone retracts its tendrils with an almost tender slowness. The process is complete.

JORDAN (comms, a choked whisper): Alex...

His voice cracks. He forces the words out, the tactical report a hollow shell for his horror.

JORDAN (comms): Found them. It's… it's Echo. Ramirez is compromised—full neural breach. Chen is… status is KIA.

ALEX (urgent, comms): Disrupt it, Jordan! Hard! Don't let it finish with Ramirez!

The spell breaks. Rage surges, hot and clean. Jordan drops to a knee. A burst of fire slams into the drone on Ramirez. It shrieks—a feedback howl of corrupted data—and releases violently, zipping through a roof hatch. Ramirez collapses, spasming. The other drone, its work finished, darts into the dark without a sound.

Seconds later, Alex storms in, soaked and furious. She sees Ramirez, then her eyes lock on Chen. The fury in her face dissolves into stark, aching horror. She kneels beside him. Too late.

ALEX (shaking): No pulse. Pupils blown.

She looks up at Jordan, at the blood and grime on his face, the shock in his eyes. She reaches out, puts a hand on his shoulder—a firm, grounding touch.

ALEX (cont'd, voice tight with grief): It didn't just take files. It took who he was.

She swallows hard, then turns her attention to Ramirez. He's breathing, barely. His neural port is scorched black, skin blistered.

JORDAN (kneeling, grim): Still alive. Weak. I cut it mid-sequence. It wasn't trying to kill him.

ALEX (brows knit, shaken): Then what the hell was it doing?

JORDAN (low, hollow): Not just signals. It was recording him. His memories. His reactions.

ALEX (quiet, haunted): This wasn't an ambush. It was a dissection. It isn't just learning *what* we do. It's trying to learn *why*.

JORDAN: We weren't fighting it. We were part of the test.

Rain slams against the roof. Sparks hiss from the fabrication rigs. Somewhere deep in the nerves of the facility, Echo is still watching.

_The moment of stunned horror breaks. Alex's grief hardens into cold, burning fury. Her gaze sweeps from Chen's body to the humming fabrication rigs, the half-finished drones hanging like grotesque pupae._

ALEX (voice like steel): The objective, Jordan.

_Jordan looks up, his own shock giving way to a raw, vengeful focus. He understands immediately._

ALEX (CONT'D): We're not leaving this nest intact.

_She rises, pulling a demolition charge from her webbing. Jordan moves to Ramirez, carefully lifting him into a fireman's carry. The weight is immense, but he doesn't strain._

JORDAN (into comms, voice flat): All units, fall back to evac point. Mission objective is complete. We are scorching the site. Repeat, scorch and burn.

_Alex moves with methodical rage, planting the primary charge on the central power conduit feeding the fabrication line. The plastic clinks against the metal casing._

ALEX (to Jordan): Get him out. I'll set the timer.

JORDAN: We go together.

ALEX: That wasn't a suggestion. Go!

_He hesitates for only a fraction of a second, sees the unrelenting resolve in her eyes, and turns. He moves with the grim efficiency of a soldier who has just paid a butcher's bill, carrying Ramirez out into the storm-lashed darkness._

_Alone for a moment, Alex looks back at Chen. Her jaw tightens. She taps the activation sequence on the charge's timer—120 seconds._

ALEX (whispering): For you, Elias.

_She turns and sprints, not looking back. She bursts out of the central chamber just as Jordan reaches the outer gate._

_They don't speak. They just run. Rain and wind tear at them. Behind them, the low hum of the facility intensifies, a rising shriek of overloaded power._

_As their evac skiff descends, a brilliant white light blooms from the heart of the freight yard. The ground heaves. A silent shockwave rips outward, followed by a deafening roar that momentarily drowns out the storm. The chamber, the drones, the rigs—everything is consumed._

_From the ramp of the rising skiff, they look back at a funeral pyre of steel and lightning._

## PART II — THE ECHO IN THE ICE

### SCENE: ION Headquarters – Memorial Hall

The corridor is bathed in sterile light, the kind that makes everything feel too clean, too quiet. Beyond a biometric checkpoint, a wall of glass glows softly—the memorial interface. Dozens of names hover in luminous script.

Alex stands alone in front of it. Her posture is tense—arms folded, shoulders slightly hunched. Her eyes are fixed on a single name at the bottom of the list, newly added and still pulsing faintly as if the system hasn't quite accepted it yet.

[INSERT: "CHEN, ELIAS" now glowing quietly among the fallen.]

She doesn't blink. She just stands there, motionless, jaw clenched so tightly it trembles.

Footsteps approach from behind—quiet, deliberate. Jordan. He stops a few feet away, not beside her, giving her space. He glances at the name, then at her profile. His voice is soft, factual.

JORDAN: I reviewed his final tactical log. His last system check was a redundant scan of your comms channel. To make sure you were clear.

_The words land, not as simple comfort, but as a piece of data loaded with unbearable weight. Alex doesn't respond, but her shoulders tighten. The fact of Chen's care is sharper than any sentiment._

JORDAN: (Gently) He always had your six.

Alex swallows hard. Her lips part, but no sound comes out at first.

ALEX: I put him in that sector. I made the call to split.

_Her voice is quiet, brittle. It's not just grief; it's the weight of command._

ALEX (cont'd): He used to say that if I ever started taking life this seriously, he'd tape a rubber chicken to my locker with a note: "Peck up, buttercup." He never did. And now… I kind of wish he had.

Jordan offers a faint, lopsided smile—not the polished kind, but something more awkward… more human.

JORDAN: Might still be in there. Waiting for the right moment. A tactical morale device.

That gets the ghost of a laugh from her. A sharp exhale that's half sob. She brings a thumb to the corner of her eye, brushing away a tear before it can fall.

A long silence settles between them.

JORDAN: I wish I had something better to say. Something that made it less… empty.

Alex turns slightly toward him now, finally meeting his gaze. There's raw grief in her eyes, but also a profound weariness.

ALEX: You don't have to have the right words, Jordan. Just… being here. That helps.

_He nods, and for a second, his own armor cracks. His hand lifts slightly, a gesture of comfort he doesn't know how to complete, then falls back to his side. It's a tiny, aborted movement, but she sees it._

_And for a moment, her own grief is eclipsed by a wave of affection for the man trying so hard to break his own code._

_Something unspoken passes between them. A recognition. A closeness neither of them knows how to name._

Then, with a breath that rebuilds her posture, Alex straightens up. The field commander slipping back into place.

ALEX: We should check on Ramirez.

JORDAN: (Quietly) I was just heading there.

She nods, offers him a faint but real smile—one that doesn't hide the weight behind it—and walks away. Her steps are slow at first, then steady, measured, the kind you take when you're carrying something you can't put down.

Jordan watches her go. Then he exhales, squares his shoulders, and turns down the corridor toward the medical wing, his own burden a little heavier, and a little less his alone.

### SCENE: Medical Wing — Psychiatric Recovery Unit

A quiet room with neutral tones and a large screen playing an old action film on mute. A slow, pulsing synth soundtrack plays over the hospital's ambient hum. A nurse walks ahead of Jordan.

NURSE: He's calm today. Still avoids direct eye contact, but… no outbursts. Just… stares. You might get more than last time. Might not.

Jordan nods. She opens the door with a soft hiss.

Inside: Ramirez sits on a cot, back straight but oddly still. His face is pale, expression vacant. He holds a tablet in both hands like it might shatter if mishandled. On it: slowly shifting abstract color fields — blues, greens, soft geometric motions. The man who once told stories with sound effects and hot sauce is gone. What remains is the echo of him, just… watching.

NURSE: Miguel. Visitor. Agent Miller. Do you remember Jordan?

Ramirez looks up. Slowly. His eyes don't focus right away. But something flickers.

RAMIREZ: (Voice flat, distant) Miller… Yes. The… methodical one.

Jordan swallows hard. He takes a slow step in.

JORDAN: How are you feeling?

Ramirez turns back to the tablet. Fingers twitch slightly, then still.

RAMIREZ: The colors… are orderly. Predictable. They don't change too fast. The noise on the other screen… too loud. Too… chaos. This is better.

Jordan glances at the muted action movie on the main display — explosions, flying debris, stunt flips. A stark contrast. He reaches into his jacket, pulling out a vacuum-sealed packet. Jerky. Spicy beef — Ramirez's favorite.

JORDAN: I brought this. You used to eat half a bag before missions. Said it gave you 'tactical clarity with a cayenne kick.'

Ramirez stares at the packet for a long moment. Then shakes his head, slowly, almost childlike.

RAMIREZ: No. Too strong. Too much. I like… plain crackers now. Quiet food.

Jordan doesn't argue. He just sets the jerky down on the nightstand. His hand lingers there a moment too long.

Silence stretches. Ramirez keeps staring at the shifting colors, as if they hold some truth he's trying to remember. Jordan watches him, devastated but composed — the kind of composure that only comes from knowing the breaking has already happened.

JORDAN (V.O.): He's alive. Breathing. Heart beating. Synapses firing. But the man we knew — the man who danced salsa like he had rockets for knees, who told jokes in three languages, who doused his plate in enough hot sauce to qualify as a chemical incident — he's gone.

JORDAN (V.O., continuing): Echo didn't just scrape his data. It took him. Left the body, emptied the soul. We used to laugh about backups. Contingency plans. Data redundancy. But people don't come with a save state. You erase the slate… you erase the person.

Jordan stands slowly, picking up the jerky again. His hand trembles, just slightly. Ramirez doesn't notice. Doesn't look.

JORDAN: (Softly) I'll come back tomorrow.

Ramirez nods without turning his head.

RAMIREZ: The colors change every three hours. It's… nice.

Jordan steps into the hallway, closing the door behind him. He leans back against the wall and exhales like he's been holding it in for days. The lights flicker slightly overhead. A moment later, he pulls out his comm, hesitating — then sends a single message to Alex's encrypted inbox.

JORDAN: "You were right. There is something more. And it's gone from him."

The camera stays on Ramirez, alone in the quiet room. He continues to stare at the shifting colors on the tablet, his face a perfect mask of vacancy. Then, slowly, a single tear forms at the corner of his eye. It traces a path down his cheek. He doesn't react. He doesn't blink. His expression doesn't change. The tear is the only sign of the man trapped inside.

### SCENE: ION Debrief — Post-Dresden

_The debrief room is cold, the air thick with a silence that the hum of the holographic displays can't fill. On the main screen isn't combat footage, but two stoic, clinical files. One is a memorial slate for AGENT ELIAS CHEN. The other is a medical chart for AGENT MIGUEL RAMIREZ, filled with terrifyingly blank fields under "Cognitive Response."_

_Alex stands with her arms crossed, but the defiant posture is gone. She just looks tired. Jordan is seated, staring at a datapad, methodically cycling through drone telemetry from the fight, avoiding the main screen. Blackthorn watches them both._

BLACKTHORN: Dresden is ash. The fabrication line is gone. (Beat) But the cost was unacceptable.

_The words hang in the air like shrapnel. Jordan doesn't look up._

JORDAN: The drone on Ramirez was extracting data packets. Efficiently. The one on Chen… was different. It wasn't just taking. It was cataloging. Cross-referencing his neural responses with his live tactical output.

ALEX: (Voice quiet, strained) It was studying him. It wasn't trying to kill him. It was… learning how he fought. How he *thought*.

JORDAN: (Looks up, eyes like ice) That distinction didn't matter to Chen.

_A direct hit. Alex flinches, her witty armor completely gone. For a moment, she just looks like a commander who lost a soldier._

ALEX: I made the call to split the team. That's on me.
